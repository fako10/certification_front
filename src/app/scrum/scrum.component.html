What is SCRUM?

Scrum helps people and teams deliver value incrementally in a collaborative manner. If you are just getting started, think of it as a way to get work done as a team in small pieces at a time, with experimentation and feedback loops along the way.

If you want to demonstrate your knowledge and understanding of the Scrum framework and how it is applied, SCRUM certifications are a great way to do so.

All of SCRUM certification tests embody the principles of Professional Scrum while providing a way for you to assess your current abilities and identify areas for improvement. Scrum has certifications covering topics that range from the fundamentals of Scrum, Scrum accountabilities, and topics beyond the Scrum framework - practices that complement Professional Scrum.

PSM I practice exam Test

PSM I certification is evidence that you have demonstrated a fundamental level of Scrum mastery. As a PSM I certification holder, you have proven that you understand Scrum as desribed in the Scrum Guide and how to apply Scrum in Scrum Teams. PSM I holders have a consistent terminology and approach to Scrum


PSM II practice exam Test

PSM II certification is evidence that you have demonstrated an advanced level of Scrum mastery, understanding of the Scrum Master accountabilities and how a scrum Team functions. As a PSM II certification holder, you have proven that you have an understanding of the principles and application of Scrum
