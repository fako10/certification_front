What is PMP Certification?

PMP® stands for Project Management Professional.

PMP is the world’s leading accreditation for those who consider project, program, or portfolio management their profession.

The PMP certification validates that you have the project leadership skills.

To obtain PMP certification, a project manager must meet certain requirements and then pass a 180-question exam. The PMP exam was created by project leaders for project leaders, so each test question can be related to real-life project management experiences.

The PMP Exam is available in the following languages: Arabic, Chinese (Simplified + Traditional), English, French, German, Hebrew, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Polish (Poland), Portuguese (Brazilian), Russian, Spanish (Mexico + Spain), and Turkish.

If you are at the early stage of your career, the Certified Associate in Project Management (CAPM) is the right qualification for you. The main difference between the two PMI certifications concerns the prerequisites to access the exam and the number of questions/timing of the exam itself.
